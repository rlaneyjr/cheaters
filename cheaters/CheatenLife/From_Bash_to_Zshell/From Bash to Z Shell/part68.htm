Entering and Editing the Command Line


In this chapter, we’ll introduce you to a wide array of nuances concerning command-line manipulation. We’ll start with some basic concepts, showing you how to choose a shell, and

how to copy and paste commands. We’ll then introduce line-editing basics using both vi and Emacs bindings, followed by a discussion of command history and navigation. We’ll conclude this chapter with a series of discussions about advanced line manipulation and editing. By its conclusion, you’ll be quite familiar with command-line navigation and best practices, knowl- edge that will undoubtedly save you considerable time and inconvenience as you interact with the shell. To begin, let’s review the options and procedures involved when choosing a shell.
